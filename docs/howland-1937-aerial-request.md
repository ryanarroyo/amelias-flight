# Archive request — 1937 aerial of Howland Island (runways / Kamakaiwi Field)

**Goal:** obtain a high-resolution scan of the 1937 aerial photograph of Howland Island showing the
three graded runways of Kamakaiwi Field (built for Amelia Earhart's planned July 1937 landing),
**with a confirmed rights/public-domain statement**, so it can be published on the tribute site
(`The Island` page) with proper credit.

**Why this is a manual request:** the image circulates online but has no verifiable, rights-clean,
directly-downloadable URL. It must be pulled from the holding archive and its rights confirmed
before publication. (We already shipped three verified PD substitutes: USFWS oblique aerial, the
1937 NARA colonists'-camp photo, and the NASA orbital view.)

---

## Where it most likely lives (cite these)

1. **NARA — RG 80, Series CF (Navy still pictures).** The verified 1937 Howland set is
   **80-CF-79868** (e.g. 80-CF-79868-1, -2, -5, -11 are on Wikimedia). An airfield/runways frame
   may be another number in the **80-CF-79868** range. Handled by the **Still Picture Branch (RDSS),
   National Archives at College Park.**
2. **NARA — RG 126, Office of Territories, "Equatorial Islands" file units** (Department of the
   Interior, Division of Territories ran the colonization project that graded the runways).
   Catalog starting point: **NAID 475739861**. Possibly RG 126 photographic subseries.
3. **NARA — RG 237 (Civil Aeronautics) / RG 80-G (Navy Bureau of Aeronautics)** as alternates for an
   aviation/aerial frame.
4. **Smithsonian Institution Archives** — photo set **"Howland I., 1937–1938"**, record
   **`fbr_item_modsi7336`** (sourced to Dept. of the Interior / U.S. Coast Guard / U.S. Navy Bureau
   of Aeronautics). Confirm per-item rights — partly Navy/CG sourced.

## On receipt — before publishing

1. **Confirm the rights statement in writing.** A 1937 U.S.-government photo is almost certainly
   public domain, but get the archive's statement; note the exact credit line they want.
2. **Save the file** to `public/photos/` (e.g. `howland-runways-1937.jpg`), **eye-verify** it shows
   the runways, and confirm it's a real JPEG.
3. **Wire it into `The Island`** — likely as the new hero, or in the closing coda beside the camp
   photo. Hand it to me with the credit line and I'll add it via `FramedPhoto` and update the
   sources note in `src/data/island.ts`.
